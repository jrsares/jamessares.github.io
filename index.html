About

I am currently a third year doctoral student in the philosophy program at Stony Brook University. I specialize in 19th and 20th century European philosophy, especially topics in phenomenology, metaphysics, and the philosophy of science. I am also a co-founder of The Society for Phenomenology and the Philosophy of Science.

I am working on two book length projects. The first is my doctoral dissertation, tentatively titled "The Impossible Dialectic: Hegelian Reflections on Time and Eternity." The second is a manuscript on the ontology of sexual difference, which engages the work of philosophers including Hegel, Gilles Deleuze, and Luce Irigaray.
